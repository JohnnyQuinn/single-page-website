Formula 1 Racing

History

First grand prix took place in 1906 in France, first official motorsport race. First championship race for F1 occurred in 1950 in England. Original cars had 1.5L supercharged or 4.5L normally aspirated engines (400 to 600 hp).

  • Car

    Modern day cars use 1.6 L V6 hybrid turbocharged engines (875 to 1,000 hp) (19k rpm) needs to be rebuilt every 500 miles Average 4 mpg Use aerodynamics to reduce air resistance and build up downforce for grip, combined downforce of about 2,500 kilograms (5,512 pounds). The minimum weight for an F1 car is 1,500 lb including the driver. Average cost $10 mil excluding research The average F1 car can go from 0 to 100 mph and then decelerate back to 0 in less than five seconds

  • Driver

    Start from other motorsports and work their way up Usually start very young with go carting Physical and mental endurance Race can be 90mins to 2 hrs Drivers loses approximately 8lb during a race Drivers experience up to 5G under braking and 3G under acceleration

  • Teams

    most teams are owned by manufacturers, others are owned privately Two drivers teams consists of R & D, technicians, and pit crew Pit crew have to Change the tires, Refuel the car, Adjust the wings, Replace damaged bodywork, Clear debris from the car's intake Fastest pit stop 1.9 seconds Teams communicate with drivers The typical annual budget of a Formula One team is about $120 million

Competition

Season consists of 15-17 races on different tracks Drivers get points based on position Driver with most points is champion Qualifying before race for grid position then race If a driver is defending his position, he can't weave back and forth. He is allowed a single blocking move and no more.
