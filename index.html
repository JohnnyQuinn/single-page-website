A Brief Intro to

Formula 1 Racing

History

- The first official motorsport race took place in 1906 in France
- The first championship race for F1 occurred in 1950 in England.
- The original cars had 1.5L supercharged or 4.5L normally aspirated engines (400 to 600 hp).

  • Cars

    Modern day cars use 1.6 L V6 hybrid turbocharged engines (875 to 1,000 hp) (19k rpm), they average 4 mpg
    The engines need to be rebuilt every 500 miles Use aerodynamics to reduce air resistance and build up downforce for grip, combined downforce of about 2,500 kilograms (5,512 pounds).
    The minimum weight for an F1 car is 1,500 lb including the driver.
    Average cost $10 mil excluding research
    The average F1 car can go from 0 to 100 mph and then decelerate back to 0 in less than five seconds

  • Drivers

    Start from other motorsports and work their way up
    Usually start very young with go carting
    Physical and mental endurance
    Race can be 90mins to 2 hrs
    Drivers loses approximately 8lb during a race
    Drivers experience up to 5G under braking and 3G under acceleration

  • Teams

    Most teams are owned by manufacturers, others are owned privately
    Each team consist of two drivers, the pit crew, race enigneers, and R & D
    Pit crew have to change the tires, refuel the car, adjust the wings, replace damaged bodywork, cear debris from the car's intake
    Fastest pit stop 1.9 seconds
    Teams communicate with drivers
    The typical annual budget of a Formula One team is about $120 million

Competition

Season consists of 15-17 races on different tracks
Drivers get points based on position
Driver with most points is champion
Qualifying before race for grid position then race
If a driver is defending his position, he can't weave back and forth. He is allowed a single blocking move and no more.
